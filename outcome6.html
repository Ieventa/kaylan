Revision

Students will be able to negotiate differences in and act with intention on feedback from readers when drafting, revising, and editing their writing.

Project 1 First Draft

Working on this assignment really helped me understand the power of revision—not just as a final clean-up step, but as an essential part of building deeper meaning in my writing. My first draft had all the research and structure, but as I revised, I realized how much stronger my argument became when I reworked certain sections for clarity, added emotional nuance, and addressed ethical concerns more directly. Through rewording ideas, reorganizing analysis, and tightening my conclusions, I saw how revision isn't just editing—it's rethinking, refining, and sometimes reshaping the whole message. This process taught me to look at my writing not as something fixed, but as something that can always evolve to better communicate what I want to say.

Project 1 Revisions

Revising my second draft pushed me to go beyond just fixing surface-level issues and really think about how each part of my paper worked together to support the overall argument. I started to notice moments where I could be more precise with my wording, more intentional with transitions, and more thoughtful in balancing evidence with analysis. This draft helped me understand that revision isn’t just about making the paper “better”—it’s about making the ideas clearer, the structure more solid, and the message more impactful. It showed me that even when something feels “done,” there’s almost always a way to dig deeper and push it further.
