Project 2 Final

This assignment pushed me to explore different genres, each with its own unique approach to conveying a message. Writing the op-ed required me to engage with logic and research, while also considering the emotional and ethical implications of AI companionship. The poem allowed me to tap into emotional expression, using rhythm and form to evoke a sense of intimacy and emptiness, demonstrating how structure can shape meaning. Lastly, creating the Instagram infographic demanded a visual and concise writing style that was both fact-driven and emotionally engaging, showing how visuals and layout can enhance communication. By working across these genres, I was able to demonstrate flexibility in my writing and the power of different rhetorical strategies to engage and inform diverse audiences.

Reader's Response: Brandt and Literacies

This particular reader's response encouraged me to engage with both analytical and creative approaches to literacy. The process of defining and reflecting on the concept of "literary sponsorship" pushed me to blend critical thinking with personal experience, allowing me to express complex ideas through a mix of academic and personal writing. I explored how AI and digital tools serve as modern literacy sponsors, using a formal, academic tone to analyze their influence, while also drawing on my own experiences to connect theory with practice. By navigating between different genres and tones, I gained a deeper understanding of how writing can shift based on purpose and audience, helping me build versatility in how I communicate ideas.
